## About Me

I'm a Computer Science M.Sc. student at **TU Berlin** (started SS 2026), working at the intersection of **deep learning, biomedical signal processing, and self-supervised representation learning**. My research focuses on building robust, interpretable AI systems for temporal and biological data — from EEG brain signals to adaptive immune receptor repertoires.

Previously, I completed my B.Sc. in Computer Science at **Shahid Beheshti University** (Tehran), where I also served as a Teaching Assistant for Machine Learning and Logic Circuits, and led AI engineering at an educational technology academy.

## 🔬 Research Interests

- **Representation Learning & Self-Supervised AI** — for temporal and biomedical signals (EEG, IMU)
- **Hybrid Deep Learning** — CNN–LSTM, CNN–Transformer architectures for multi-channel time-series classification
- **Explainable & Trustworthy AI (XAI)** — interpretability in applied clinical and educational settings
- **Computational Immunology** — AIRR repertoire simulation and analysis (IGoR, OLGA, ImmuneSIM)
- **AI in Education** — adaptive learning systems and cognitive assessment

## 🚀 Active Research Projects

### 🧠 Hybrid CNN–LSTM Framework for EEG Classification
`Oct 2024 – Present`

Building hybrid CNN, LSTM, and Transformer architectures for multi-channel EEG classification. Includes full preprocessing pipelines: normalization, segmentation, artifact removal, and class labeling.

→ *[View Repository](https://github.com/Yasamin-Alemzadeh)*

---

### ⏱ Contrastive Learning for Time-Series Representation
`Jun 2024 – Present`

Research pipeline integrating **SimCLR** with Transformer-based forecasting. Covers augmentation strategies, cross-subject validation, and temporal dependency modeling.

→ *[View Repository](https://github.com/Yasamin-Alemzadeh)*

---

### 🧬 Synthetic Immune Repertoire Data Pipeline
`Feb 2025 – Present`

Multi-tool bioinformatics pipeline integrating **IGoR, OLGA, AIRRSHIP, ImmuneSIM, and LigoSim** for synthetic immune data generation, with downstream analysis via MiXCR, scRepertoire, and SONIA.
